Unified AI and Data Governance

As part of my work at KHC – Amsterdam Center of Excellence, I led initiatives to unify AI and data governance across global projects. This involved designing and implementing advanced stewardship and data quality frameworks, ensuring compliance with international standards, and enabling secure, scalable AI solutions for a Forbes 500 company. My role required close collaboration with multidisciplinary teams in the US, Europe, and Asia, focusing on harmonizing data assets and governance processes to support enterprise-wide AI adoption.

Global Data Stewardship

I have been responsible for establishing global data stewardship programs, developing policies and best practices for data quality, lineage, and lifecycle management. My work included mentoring data stewards, implementing stewardship tools, and fostering a culture of accountability and data-driven decision-making across large organizations. These efforts ensured that data assets were reliable, well-documented, and aligned with business objectives.

Anonimization for Healthcare AI

In healthcare projects, I designed and implemented anonymization pipelines to enable the safe use of sensitive patient data for AI and analytics. Leveraging advanced de-identification techniques and privacy-preserving algorithms, I ensured compliance with GDPR and HIPAA regulations. This work enabled research and innovation in healthcare AI while protecting patient privacy and maintaining data utility for machine learning models.

Covid Vaccines Effectiveness (CoVE)

Project Website

As Core Scientific Lead, I designed and coordinated this international project involving multiple European universities to evaluate the effectiveness of heterologous and booster COVID-19 vaccination schedules in five countries. Our research used a cohort approach, focusing on both children and adults who completed a full primary vaccination regimen.

I led the development of a robust data pipeline, ensuring data from all partners adhered to a synthetic common data model and harmonized semantic libraries. This enabled consistent analysis and interpretation of health data across diverse international contexts.

Covid-19 Scenarios (by Neherlab)

My contribution to the model, was to implement a new flux in the SIR model called “Palliative Flux”. Coding with Typescript, Python and Node.js and involving many kinds of data validations and complex epidemiology modelling.

To accomplish this, I had to set cloud environments (dev and production) with Continuous Integration and Delivery of software, so statisticians could work on local models.

Our environment was equipped with a data parser, to capture data from our local government dashboards and after processing than, I have customized the calculator to build specific scenarios for 26 local regions.

This was a work group with collaboration from statistics, medicine and computer science teachers from the federal university along with state authorities.

References: https://www.covid19-scenarios.org
